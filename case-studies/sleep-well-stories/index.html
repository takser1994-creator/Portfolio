AI bedtime story app parents trust

Sleep Well Stories is an iOS app that generates personalised bedtime stories for kids aged 3–12 in under a minute. Parents describe what the story should be about, answer a few guided questions, and the app writes and narrates it aloud.

User persona

Maya is a working mother of two who has tried other bedtime apps and put them aside. She represents the parent we designed the product around — time-pressed, attentive, and willing to pay when the experience earns it.

Motivation

Those twenty minutes before bed are the calmest part of her day, and she wants them to count. The kids should hear their own names, recognize the park down the street, their own dog, not characters pulled from a generic library.

Expectations

A fast start, story playing inside a minute, no sign-up wall. A narrator that won't cut out halfway through. Content she doesn't have to pre-read. And on the nights her kids are still awake enough to want a say, the option to let them steer the story themselves.

User feedback findings

I spoke with eight parents about the bedtime apps they'd already tried: what slowed them down, what disappointed their kid, and what their kid still asked for that the app couldn't give. Three patterns carried directly into the early product priorities.

The path to a first story was long and complicated

Account creation, child profile setup, voice selection, and free-tier prompts pushed the first listen well past the bedtime window. Parents described putting the app down before a single story had played.

Lack of control over the story
disappointed children

Parents recalled stories that landed wrong: an unexpected theme, an unfamiliar setting, a tone that didn't fit the night. With no way to preview or steer what was coming, the disappointment hit the child directly.

Children wanted to create their
own heroes and places

Beyond picking from preset characters, children asked to invent their own: a friend's name, a familiar pet, the park down the street. Existing apps stopped at the template, so the request went unanswered.

A first story in seconds

Onboarding shrank to a single screen: no account, no preference quiz, no paywall. The first story starts playing within sixty seconds of opening the app.

Search, filters, and characters throughout the app

Search and filters are built for quick access to the right story, and the characters appear across the app — on the home screen, in the library, in settings — not only inside the scenes themselves

Where the project landed

Sleep Well Stories shipped to the App Store as a free download with a €2.99 monthly subscription. The build covers what this case study is built around — fast start, parent-controlled creation, and a hero the child invents.

What shipped

Available on the App Store in English and German, free to download with a €2.99 monthly subscription. The build covers the create flow that gets a family to a story in under sixty seconds, the read mode where the invented hero appears as both illustration and narrated voice, and the multi-child library that keeps each sibling’s stories separate.

What the project taught me

The biggest thing I underestimated was how much of the bedtime experience happens before the story even starts. Every screen between opening the app and the first words was a place a child could lose interest — and that is where the design had to fight hardest. Next time I would test earlier, with parents and children together.
